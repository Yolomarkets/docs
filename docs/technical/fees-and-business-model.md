# Fees and Business Model

YOLO Markets is not just a market surface. It is also a business.

## The Basic Model

At a high level, the product monetizes through trading activity.

That is the simplest and cleanest business model for a market product built around active participation: if users trade and the market is useful, the product earns.

## How To Think About Fees

The important concept is not a specific fee table on this page. It is the role fees play in the system.

Fees do three things:

- monetize market usage
- create treasury value for the platform
- tie business performance to actual trading demand

This is a healthier model than relying only on speculative attention without market usage.

## Technical Reality

Under the hood, the stack tracks and aggregates collected fees at the exchange and engine level, then exposes a path for the system to inspect and collect them.

That means fee capture is not just a conceptual slide-deck idea. It is part of the real operating system around the markets.

## Why This Fits the Product

YOLO is building a trading venue around attention-driven markets.

If the venue is useful, users trade.
If users trade, the platform captures fees.
If the platform captures fees, the business model compounds with product adoption.

That alignment is important. It means the business model grows from actual market usage, not from a disconnected monetization layer.
