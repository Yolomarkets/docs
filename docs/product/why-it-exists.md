# Why It Exists

Attention is one of the most valuable resources on the internet, but it has historically been hard to trade directly.

People form strong opinions about:

- which topics will dominate timelines
- which narratives will gain momentum
- which cultural moments will persist
- which internet-native themes will fade

## The Gap

Traditional financial markets are built for companies, commodities, rates, and established asset classes.

Prediction markets are powerful for discrete questions, but they are not always the right tool for open-ended narrative trading. A topic can trend, cool off, re-accelerate, fragment, or evolve. That is often closer to a continuous market than a one-time yes/no resolution.

## The Product Thesis

The product thesis is simple:

- attention has economic value
- narratives have market behavior
- conviction about internet culture can be priced

If users can express that conviction through a market, then attention stops being something people only talk about and becomes something they can trade.

## Why Perps

Perp-style markets are a strong fit because they support an ongoing market rather than a single fixed expiry. That matters for topics that keep evolving in real time.

Instead of waiting for one terminal outcome, users can:

- go long if they think a topic is strengthening
- go short if they think it is losing momentum
- manage a position as the narrative changes

## What YOLO Is Trying To Build

YOLO Markets is trying to become a market layer for narratives, topics, attention, and internet culture.
