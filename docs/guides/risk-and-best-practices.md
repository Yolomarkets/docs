# Risk and Best Practices

YOLO Markets is designed to be expressive and fast. That also means users should approach it with discipline.

## The Main Risk Profile

These are attention-driven perp markets. That combines:

- leverage and margin risk
- funding risk
- market-liquidity risk
- narrative volatility

Even when the interface feels familiar, the underlying market behavior can change quickly.

## Best Practices for New Users

Start with:

- small size
- topics you actually understand
- short feedback loops
- clear exit ideas

A user who understands the topic but sizes recklessly is still exposed. A user who sizes correctly but does not understand the topic is also exposed.

## Respect Funding

Funding is easy to ignore when a trade is moving in your favor, but it matters most when a position stays open.

Before holding longer, ask:

- am I paying to hold this view?
- is the market overstretched relative to the index?
- does this still make sense after funding cost?

## Respect Narrative Reflexivity

Narrative markets can overshoot, snap back, and react to social signals very quickly.

That means:

- obvious trades can become crowded
- price can move before the narrative feels "fully proven"
- late entries can be worse than wrong ideas

## The Practical Standard

Trade YOLO markets like a product that rewards understanding, timing, and discipline.

Do not assume:

- beta convenience means low risk
- points justify poor entries
- strong conviction removes the need for risk management

The best users will treat the product as a serious trading system, even while it is still in beta.
